Definition:
Xenoestrogens are a diverse class of natural and artificial chemicals that mimic or interfere with the function of the hormone estrogen. They may bind to estrogen receptors, alter endocrine signaling, or disrupt normal reproductive development. Common sources include plastics (e.g., BPA, phthalates), pesticides, and certain industrial byproducts.
