## Xenoestrogen

*English*

**Part of Speech** | **IPA (RP & GenAm)** /ˌzɛnəʊˈɛstrədʒən/ or /ˌziːnoʊˈɛstrədʒən/  
**Japanese (Katakana)** ゼノウエストロジェン / ズィノウエストロジェン

**Rhymes** -estrogen  

> “Any of a group of natural or synthetic compounds showing hormone-like activity that acts as an estrogen.”

---

### Definition

Xenoestrogens are a diverse class of **natural and artificial chemicals** that mimic or interfere with the function of the hormone *estrogen*.  
They may bind to estrogen receptors, alter endocrine signaling, or disrupt normal reproductive development.  
Common sources include **plastics** (e.g., BPA, phthalates), **pesticides**, and certain **industrial byproducts**.

### Example Sentence

Wastewater-borne *xenoestrogens* have been implicated in endocrine disruption across multiple species.

---

### Etymology

From Greek *xenos* “foreign” + *estrogen* — literally “foreign estrogen.”

---

### Related Terms

- **Phytoestrogen** — plant-derived estrogen-like compound  
- **Endocrine disruptor** — substance that interferes with hormonal systems  
- **Hormone mimic** — molecule imitating natural hormone action
